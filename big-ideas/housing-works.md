# Housing Works

## Work-to-Own Housing Program

### Core Concept
A comprehensive program that combines immediate employment, skills training, and a path to homeownership for all participants, with special focus on helping unhoused individuals transition to stable housing and employment.

### Program Components

#### Immediate Employment
- Minimum wage paid from day one - no volunteer requirements
- Open to all, including currently unhoused individuals
- No experience necessary - training provided
- Full-time and part-time positions available

#### Home Credits System
- Participants earn "home credits" in addition to wages
- Credits accumulate based on hours worked
- Credits can be applied toward down payment on program-built homes
- Transferable between family members working in program

#### Skills Training
- On-the-job training from skilled construction workers
- Apprenticeship opportunities in various trades
- Certification programs for specialized skills
- Resume building and job placement assistance

#### Temporary Housing
- On-site temporary housing for workers who need it
- Basic facilities with dignity and privacy
- Transition support to permanent housing
- Community spaces for workers and families

#### Purchase Priority System
- Workers who helped build homes get first purchase rights
- Forgivable mortgage program for owner-occupants
- 10-year residency requirement for full forgiveness
- Protections against speculation and flipping

### Benefits

#### For Participants
- Immediate income at fair wages
- Path to homeownership without traditional barriers
- Marketable skills training
- Community building with fellow workers
- Dignity of meaningful work

#### For Communities
- Addresses housing shortage
- Reduces homelessness through employment
- Creates affordable homeownership opportunities
- Builds skilled local workforce
- Strengthens community bonds

### Implementation Considerations

#### Funding Sources
- Federal housing grants
- State and local housing bonds
- Public-private partnerships
- Social impact investments
- Land value capture mechanisms

#### Partner Organizations
- Local unions for skilled worker trainers
- Community colleges for certification programs
- Nonprofit housing developers
- Social service organizations
- Local employers for job placement

#### Quality Standards
- Homes built to high energy efficiency standards
- Universal design principles for accessibility
- Local architecture reflecting community character
- Green building materials and practices
- Long-term maintenance planning

### Pilot Program Structure

#### Phase 1: Small Scale (6 months)
- 10-20 workers
- 5 homes
- Test systems and processes
- Gather participant feedback

#### Phase 2: Expansion (1 year)
- 50-100 workers
- 25 homes
- Multiple work sites
- Refined training programs

#### Phase 3: Full Implementation (Ongoing)
- 200+ workers
- 100+ homes annually
- Self-sustaining through home sales
- Replicable model for other communities

### Success Metrics
- Number of participants employed
- Wages and credits earned
- Homes completed
- Participants who purchase homes
- Long-term homeownership retention
- Skills certifications earned
- Participants placed in permanent jobs
- Reduction in local homelessness

### Similar Models
- Habitat for Humanity (volunteer model)
- YouthBuild (youth-focused)
- Community land trusts
- Cooperative housing development
- Self-help housing programs

### Key Differences
- Immediate fair wages, not volunteer
- Open to all, not just low-income qualified
- Forgivable mortgage structure
- Integrated temporary housing
- Focus on participant dignity and choice
