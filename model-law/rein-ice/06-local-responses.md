# Local Executive Actions & Rapid Responses

Quick-action measures that can be implemented by mayors and local executives without full legislative process.

---

## Minneapolis Executive Order 2025-02 (ICE Property Ban)

**Jurisdiction:** Minneapolis, Minnesota
**Legal Citation:** Executive Order 2025-02
**Status:** Enacted 2025

**Key Provisions:**
- Bans ICE from using city-owned property for enforcement operations
- Prohibits city cooperation with federal immigration raids
- Directs city departments to limit information sharing with ICE

**Why It Matters:** Demonstrates what executive action can accomplish quickly. Model for mayors in other cities facing immediate threats.

**Links:**
- [Original Source (City of Minneapolis)](https://www.minneapolismn.gov/government/mayor/executive-orders/executive-order-2025-02/)

## Executive Actions vs. Ordinances

| Aspect | Executive Order | Ordinance |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| Speed | Immediate | Weeks/months |
| Authority | Mayor only | City council |
| Durability | Can be reversed by next mayor | Requires council action to repeal |
| Scope | City operations | Broader |
| Best For | Rapid response | Long-term policy |

---

## What Mayors Can Do Immediately

**Property & Facilities:**
- Ban ICE from city-owned buildings
- Prohibit use of city property for detention
- Restrict access to city jails/holding facilities

**City Employees:**
- Direct employees not to inquire about immigration status
- Prohibit voluntary cooperation with ICE
- Establish protocols for responding to ICE requests

**Information:**
- Limit data sharing with federal agencies
- Require warrants for information release
- Train employees on privacy protocols

**Public Spaces:**
- Declare schools, libraries, hospitals as "safe zones"
- Post signs about immigration enforcement limits
- Create community notification systems

---

## Building from Executive Action to Legislation

1. **Immediate**: Mayor issues executive order
2. **Documentation**: Track implementation and community impact
3. **Coalition**: Build support among council members
4. **Draft**: Convert executive order into ordinance language
5. **Passage**: Council adopts permanent ordinance
6. **Defense**: Prepare for legal/political challenges
